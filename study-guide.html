Decade Event People Involved Significance
1760s Stamp Act (1765) British Parliament, American colonists Tax imposed by the British on American colonists, leading to widespread protests and contributing to the American Revolution.
Townshend Acts (1767) Charles Townshend, American colonists Additional taxes imposed on the colonies, fueling resentment toward British rule and pushing the colonies toward revolution.
Boston Massacre (1770) British soldiers, American colonists Violent confrontation leading to the deaths of five colonists and escalating tensions between the two sides.
Boston Tea Party (1773) Sons of Liberty, British government Key event leading to the American Revolution, as colonists protested against British taxation by dumping tea into Boston Harbor.
First Continental Congress (1774) George Washington, John Adams, Patrick Henry, Samuel Adams Beginning of coordinated colonial resistance against British rule, eventually leading to the American Revolution.
Decade Event People Involved Significance
1770s American Revolution (1775-1783) George Washington, Thomas Jefferson, Benjamin Franklin, John Adams War that led to the colonies gaining independence from British rule, resulting in the formation of the United States.
Declaration of Independence (1776) Thomas Jefferson, John Adams, Benjamin Franklin, Continental Congress Document that formally declared the colonies' independence from Britain and established the United States as a new nation.
Battle of Saratoga (1777) Horatio Gates, Benedict Arnold, John Burgoyne Major turning point in the American Revolution; the American victory convinced France to join the war as an American ally.
Valley Forge (1777-1778) George Washington, Continental Army A difficult winter encampment for the Continental Army; the experience strengthened the army and demonstrated their resilience.
Articles of Confederation (ratified in 1781) Continental Congress, American states First constitution of the United States; it highlighted the weaknesses of a weak central government and paved the way for the current U.S. Constitution.
Decade Event People Involved Significance
1780s Treaty of Paris (1783) Benjamin Franklin, John Adams, John Jay, David Hartley Officially ended the American Revolution and recognized the independence of the United States from Britain.
Annapolis Convention (1786) Founding Fathers, including James Madison and Alexander Hamilton Meeting to address issues related to the Articles of Confederation, which led to the call for the Constitutional Convention.
Constitutional Convention (1787) George Washington, James Madison, Benjamin Franklin, Alexander Hamilton, Thomas Jefferson Gathering to draft a new constitution for the United States, leading to the creation of the U.S. Constitution.
Ratification of the U.S. Constitution (1788) Founding Fathers, American states Established the United States' current system of government and replaced the Articles of Confederation.
First Presidential Election (1788-1789) George Washington, John Adams Marked the beginning of the United States' political system, with George Washington being elected as the first president.
Decade Event People Involved Significance
1790s Bill of Rights (1791) James Madison, U.S. Congress First ten amendments to the U.S. Constitution, guaranteeing individual liberties and protecting states' rights.
First Bank of the United States (1791) Alexander Hamilton, U.S. Congress Established to manage the country's finances, support public credit, and provide a stable national currency.
Whiskey Rebellion (1794) George Washington, Alexander Hamilton, Western Pennsylvania farmers A tax protest that demonstrated the power of the new federal government to enforce laws and maintain order.
Jay's Treaty (1794) John Jay, George Washington, British government A treaty that resolved outstanding issues between the United States and Britain, averting a potential war.
Alien and Sedition Acts (1798) John Adams, U.S. Congress Controversial laws that restricted immigrants' rights and limited free speech, leading to the Virginia and Kentucky Resolutions.
Decade Event People Involved Significance
1800-1810 Election of 1800 (1800) Thomas Jefferson, John Adams, Aaron Burr Marked the first peaceful transfer of power between political parties in the U.S. and led to the 12th Amendment.
Marbury v. Madison (1803) William Marbury, James Madison, Chief Justice John Marshall Established the principle of judicial review, giving the Supreme Court the power to declare laws unconstitutional.
Louisiana Purchase (1803) Thomas Jefferson, Napoleon Bonaparte, Robert Livingston, James Monroe Doubled the size of the United States, opened vast new territories for settlement, and secured the Mississippi River for western trade.
Lewis and Clark Expedition (1804-1806) Meriwether Lewis, William Clark, Sacagawea Explored and mapped the newly acquired western territory, established relationships with Native American tribes, and gathered valuable scientific data.
Embargo Act (1807) Thomas Jefferson, U.S. Congress Aimed to protect U.S. interests by prohibiting trade with foreign nations, but had negative economic effects and contributed to tensions leading to the War of 1812.
Decade Event People Involved Significance
1820s Missouri Compromise (1820) Henry Clay, U.S. Congress Addressed the issue of slavery in new territories, maintaining a balance between slave and free states, and temporarily reducing sectional tensions.
Monroe Doctrine (1823) James Monroe, John Quincy Adams A key foreign policy statement, asserting that the Western Hemisphere was closed to further European colonization and intervention.
Erie Canal (completed in 1825) DeWitt Clinton, engineers, and laborers Significantly reduced transportation costs, spurred economic growth in the Northeast and Midwest, and contributed to New York City's rise as a major port.
Indian Removal Act (1830) Andrew Jackson, U.S. Congress Authorized the forced relocation of Native American tribes from their ancestral lands in the southeastern United States to lands west of the Mississippi River.
First railroad in the U.S. (1826) John Stevens, the Granite Railway Company Marked the beginning of the railroad industry in the U.S., which revolutionized transportation, commerce, and westward expansion.
Decade Event People Involved Significance
1830s Indian Removal Act (1830) Andrew Jackson, U.S. Congress Authorized the forced relocation of Native American tribes from their ancestral lands in the southeastern United States to lands west of the Mississippi River.
Trail of Tears (1831-1838) Andrew Jackson, Cherokee, Choctaw, Chickasaw, Creek, and Seminole tribes Forced relocation of Native American tribes, resulting in the deaths of thousands and the loss of their ancestral lands.
Nullification Crisis (1832-1833) Andrew Jackson, John C. Calhoun, South Carolina Highlighted the tensions between states' rights and federal authority, as South Carolina threatened to secede over a federal tariff.
Second Bank of the United States (vetoed in 1832) Andrew Jackson, Nicholas Biddle, U.S. Congress President Jackson's veto of the bank's recharter highlighted the debate over the role of the federal government in the economy.
Texas Revolution (1835-1836) Stephen F. Austin, Sam Houston, Santa Anna Conflict between American settlers in Texas and the Mexican government, leading to the establishment of the Republic of Texas and eventually its annexation by the United States.
Decade Event People Involved Significance
1840s Manifest Destiny (1840s) John L. O'Sullivan, James K. Polk, American settlers The belief in the United States' divine mission to expand westward, driving territorial acquisition and settlement.
Oregon Trail (1840s) American pioneers, missionaries, and settlers Major route for westward migration, allowing for the settlement of the Oregon Territory and contributing to westward expansion.
Mexican-American War (1846-1848) James K. Polk, Zachary Taylor, Winfield Scott, Santa Anna Resulted in the acquisition of large portions of Mexican territory, including California and the Southwest, and intensified sectional tensions over slavery.
Seneca Falls Convention (1848) Elizabeth Cady Stanton, Lucretia Mott, Frederick Douglass The first women's rights convention, marking the beginning of the organized women's rights movement in the United States.
California Gold Rush (1848-1855) James W. Marshall, John Sutter, prospectors Attracted a massive influx of settlers to California, leading to rapid economic and demographic growth and the eventual admission of California as a state.
