Decade Event People Involved Significance
1760s Stamp Act (1765) British Parliament, American colonists Tax imposed by the British on American colonists, leading to widespread protests and contributing to the American Revolution.
Townshend Acts (1767) Charles Townshend, American colonists Additional taxes imposed on the colonies, fueling resentment toward British rule and pushing the colonies toward revolution.
Boston Massacre (1770) British soldiers, American colonists Violent confrontation leading to the deaths of five colonists and escalating tensions between the two sides.
Boston Tea Party (1773) Sons of Liberty, British government Key event leading to the American Revolution, as colonists protested against British taxation by dumping tea into Boston Harbor.
First Continental Congress (1774) George Washington, John Adams, Patrick Henry, Samuel Adams Beginning of coordinated colonial resistance against British rule, eventually leading to the American Revolution.
Decade Event People Involved Significance
1770s American Revolution (1775-1783) George Washington, Thomas Jefferson, Benjamin Franklin, John Adams War that led to the colonies gaining independence from British rule, resulting in the formation of the United States.
Declaration of Independence (1776) Thomas Jefferson, John Adams, Benjamin Franklin, Continental Congress Document that formally declared the colonies' independence from Britain and established the United States as a new nation.
Battle of Saratoga (1777) Horatio Gates, Benedict Arnold, John Burgoyne Major turning point in the American Revolution; the American victory convinced France to join the war as an American ally.
Valley Forge (1777-1778) George Washington, Continental Army A difficult winter encampment for the Continental Army; the experience strengthened the army and demonstrated their resilience.
Articles of Confederation (ratified in 1781) Continental Congress, American states First constitution of the United States; it highlighted the weaknesses of a weak central government and paved the way for the current U.S. Constitution.
Decade Event People Involved Significance
1780s Treaty of Paris (1783) Benjamin Franklin, John Adams, John Jay, David Hartley Officially ended the American Revolution and recognized the independence of the United States from Britain.
Annapolis Convention (1786) Founding Fathers, including James Madison and Alexander Hamilton Meeting to address issues related to the Articles of Confederation, which led to the call for the Constitutional Convention.
Constitutional Convention (1787) George Washington, James Madison, Benjamin Franklin, Alexander Hamilton, Thomas Jefferson Gathering to draft a new constitution for the United States, leading to the creation of the U.S. Constitution.
Ratification of the U.S. Constitution (1788) Founding Fathers, American states Established the United States' current system of government and replaced the Articles of Confederation.
First Presidential Election (1788-1789) George Washington, John Adams Marked the beginning of the United States' political system, with George Washington being elected as the first president.
Decade Event People Involved Significance
1790s Bill of Rights (1791) James Madison, U.S. Congress First ten amendments to the U.S. Constitution, guaranteeing individual liberties and protecting states' rights.
First Bank of the United States (1791) Alexander Hamilton, U.S. Congress Established to manage the country's finances, support public credit, and provide a stable national currency.
Whiskey Rebellion (1794) George Washington, Alexander Hamilton, Western Pennsylvania farmers A tax protest that demonstrated the power of the new federal government to enforce laws and maintain order.
Jay's Treaty (1794) John Jay, George Washington, British government A treaty that resolved outstanding issues between the United States and Britain, averting a potential war.
Alien and Sedition Acts (1798) John Adams, U.S. Congress Controversial laws that restricted immigrants' rights and limited free speech, leading to the Virginia and Kentucky Resolutions.
